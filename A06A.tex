\item{\textbf{Question 2.75:}}\\

Discuss the continuity of the function f , where f is defined by,\\
$

a) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 3 & 0 \le x \le 1\\
4 & 0 < x \le 3\\
5 & 3 \le x \le 10\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

b) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 2x & x < 0\\
0 & 0 \le x \le 1\\
4x & x > 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

c) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 -2 & x < -1\\
2x & -1 \le x \le 1\\
2 & x > 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

a) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 3 & 0 \le x \le 1\\
4 & 0 < x \le 3\\
5 & 3 \le x \le 10\\
\end{array}
\right. \]
$\\

As f(x) is the mixture of constant functions \therefore{} &it will be continuous at every point except points at which  constant functions are changing  their behaviour i.e, 1,3.\\

for 1:\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x)=3\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)=4\\

or, \hspace{3 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x)\neq lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)\\

 \therefore{} &f(x) is not continuous at x=1.\\
 
 Similarly for x=3.\\
 
b) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 2x & x < 0\\
0 & 0 \le x \le 1\\
4x & x > 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

for f(x) to be continuous at every point, it should be continuous at 0 and 1.\\
$
for 0:\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to0^{-}}f(x)=2x=0\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to0^{+}}f(x)=0\\

or, \hspace{3 cm}lim_{x\to0^{-}}f(x)=lim_{x\to0^{+}}f(x)\\

f(x) is continuous at x=0.\\

for 1:\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x)=0\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)=4x=4\\

or, \hspace{3 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x) \neq lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)\\

f(x) is not continuous at x=1.\\
$


c) \[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
 -2 & x < -1\\
2x & -1 \le x \le 1\\
2 & x > 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

for f(x) to be continuous at all points it should be continuous at -1 and 1.\\
$

for -1:\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to-1^{-}}f(x)=-2\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to-1^{+}}f(x)=2x=-2\\

or, \hspace{3 cm}lim_{x\to-1^{-}}f(x)=lim_{x\to-1^{+}}f(x)\\

f(x) is continuous at x=-1.\\

for 1:\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x)=2x=2\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)=2\\

or, \hspace{3 cm}lim_{x\to1^{-}}f(x)=lim_{x\to1^{+}}f(x)\\

f(x) is continuous at all points.\\
$

\item{\textbf{Question 2.76:}}\\

Find the relationship between a and b so that the function defined by,\\
 
\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
ax+1 & x \le 1\\
bx+3 & x > 3\\
\end{array}
\right. \]
$\\

is continuous at x=3.\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
ax+1 & x \le 1\\
bx+3 & x > 3\\
\end{array}
\right. \]
$\\

for f(x) to be continuous at $x=3$.\\

$
\hspace{3.5 cm}\lim_{x\to3^{+}}f(x)=\lim_{x\to3^{-}}f(x)$.\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$b(3)+3=a(3)+1$.\\

Hence, \hspace{3 cm}$(a-b)=2/3$\\
